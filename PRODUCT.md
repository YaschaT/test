# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Self-directed JLPT learners studying independently toward N5→N4 (with an eye toward eventually covering
the full N5-through-N1 range), fitting study into short daily sessions. Their job-to-be-done on any given
screen is progressing toward genuine exam readiness — reviewing due SRS cards, learning new
vocabulary/kanji, or checking how close they are to being ready for the next level.

## Product Purpose

Kotobox helps learners build real, exam-ready Japanese proficiency across six skills (grammar, vocabulary,
kanji, reading, listening, speaking) through spaced-repetition practice, daily study planning, and clear
signals of how close they are to exam readiness. Success is measured by genuine mastery and readiness
signals — not by generic engagement metrics alone.

## Brand Personality

Laid-back and mascot-led rather than flashy or loud — a calm study companion, not a carnival. Gamified
elements (streaks, XP, level-ups, badges) exist to support motivation but must never overshadow the serious
work of exam preparation. Not childish. Not a direct clone of any single competitor.

## Anti-references

- Overly flashy, loud gamified apps where the "game" overshadows the studying.
- Generic gray/white corporate SaaS dashboards.
- Direct visual cloning of Duolingo or any other single competitor — inspiration without imitation.

## Design Principles

- **Progress over decoration.** Gamified elements (streaks, XP, level-ups, badges, confetti) support real
  exam-readiness signals; they never replace or outshine them.
- **Calm confidence, not carnival.** The mascot is a laid-back companion. Visual energy stays measured and
  premium, not loud or overstimulating.
- **Real data, always.** Every number shown is genuinely derived from actual user progress — never
  fabricated placeholder content, and no dead buttons or fake features.
- **Exam-readiness is the north star.** Features and copy tie back to genuine JLPT readiness (mastery
  signals, remaining content, review load) rather than generic engagement-only metrics.
- **Bilingual by default.** English + Dutch text pairing throughout, an established convention across every
  module.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA baseline: contrast ratios, full keyboard navigation, visible focus states. Reduced motion is a
hard requirement, not a nice-to-have — every animation needs a `prefers-reduced-motion` alternative (already
implemented app-wide via a dedicated CSS media query block).
